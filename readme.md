## 📘 Part A: Theory & Definitions

### 1️⃣ Types of Data

#### 🔢 Numerical Data
- Represents measurable quantities
- Can perform mathematical operations

**Examples:**
- Age_of_Household_Head
- Household_Income
- Family_Size

#### 🏷 Categorical Data
- Represents labels or categories

**Examples:**
- Education_Level
- Owns_House
- Urban_Rural

---

### 2️⃣ Types of Statistics

#### 📈 Descriptive Statistics
- Summarizes and describes data
- Uses mean, median, charts, tables

**Example:** Average household income

#### 🔮 Inferential Statistics
- Draws conclusions about a population from a sample
- Uses probability and hypothesis testing

---

### 3️⃣ Descriptive Statistics (Definition)
Descriptive statistics help in **organizing, summarizing, and presenting data** meaningfully.

✔ Measures of Central Tendency  
✔ Measures of Dispersion  
✔ Graphical Representations

## 📐 Measures of Central Tendency

| Measure | Meaning | Use Case |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **Mean** | Average value | Overall income level |
| **Median** | Middle value | Best for skewed income |
| **Mode** | Most frequent value | Common income group |

---

## 📏 Measures of Dispersion

| Measure | Meaning |
|-------|--------|
| **Range** | Max − Min |
| **Variance** | Spread from mean |
| **Standard Deviation** | Average deviation |

---

## 📊 Distribution & Statistical Concepts

### 🔔 Gaussian (Normal) Distribution
- Symmetrical, bell‑shaped curve

**Formula:**
\[ f(x)= \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}} \]

---

### 📉 Log‑Normal Distribution
- Data is positively skewed
- Income data often follows this

---

### 📐 3‑Sigma Rule (Empirical Rule)

| Range | Coverage |
|------|----------|
| μ ± 1σ | 68% |
| μ ± 2σ | 95% |
| μ ± 3σ | 99.7% |

---

### 📊 Percentiles & Quartiles
- **Percentiles:** Position of a value in data
- **Quartiles:** Divide data into 4 equal parts

---

### 📦 Five Number Summary

| Metric | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Minimum | Lowest value |
| Q1 | 25th percentile |
| Median | 50th percentile |
| Q3 | 75th percentile |
| Maximum | Highest value |

---

### ↗ Skewness

| Type | Shape |
|----|-------|
| Positive | Right‑skewed (income) |
| Negative | Left‑skewed |

---

### 🧮 Kurtosis

| Type | Interpretation |
|-----|---------------|
| Mesokurtic | Normal |
| Leptokurtic | Heavy tails |
| Platykurtic | Flat |
